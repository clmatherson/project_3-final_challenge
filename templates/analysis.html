Brief Description

We knew going into this project it would be very challenging because object detection and machine learning on images was completely new to the entire group. During the two weeks there were many challenges that needed to be overcome, and most were.

Model Analysis

Having good images for our model is critical for training with the need for different views and orientation for training. Because we lacked good variety of images for our model we had mixed results with our loss function as well as our accuracy during object-detection demonstration. Both could be improved with a second attempt at creating our images used for training.
There are multiple models to choose from the Tensorflow Github page. For this project Model ssd_mobilenet_v2_pfnlite_320x320_coco17_tpu-8 was chosen and that model was trained on 5, 10, and 20 words. The loss comparison are compared in the graphs below. All 3 models were plotted on the same graph per easy comparison. Clearly the fewer words the better accuracy. The lowest model loss acheived was 0.18 and ideally a loss lower than 0.10 is ideal.
The individual loss graphs are displayed below. The 20-word model starts at a much higher Loss and has higher variation than with the two models with fewer words. Our object detection threshold was set at 0.1 to increase our detection when displaying in CV2. This was much lower than we would have liked and could be improved with better image variety as stated previously.

Recommendations for improvement

Model Training

Training takes 6 to 20 hours to train a model depending on computer hardware. It did not appear that the number of pictures affected the training significantly and a high-end gaming computer performs much better than standard desktop computer. Further increase in speed is possible with Nvida GPUs with CUDA and cuDNN. We were unable to get this to work because of version conflicts between the newer version of Tensorflow we were using and the older version of CUDA and CUDnn.

Improve Model Accuracy by Reducing Losses

  • Try different Tensorflow models that increase performance instead of speed of training
  • Improve model predicting by improving picture variety
    • increasing angle of pose and lighting variation
  • Create a standard for cropping pictures
  • Test affects on affects running the model individuals not trained and tested
    • Variety of pose angles and positioning
    • Tighter bounding boxes
  • Train longer by using 30,000 steps
  • Make into an app/website
